The Prophet's Era (570-632 CE)

The period that marked the beginning of the Quran revelation, establishing the foundations of Islamic faith and society.

Significant Events

  • The First Revelation (610 CE)
  • Hijra to Medina (622 CE)
  • The Constitution of Medina
  • Conquest of Mecca (630 CE)

Rashidun Caliphate (632–661 CE)

This era is considered the golden age of Islamic leadership, as the caliphs ruled based on the Prophet's teachings and principles of justice and consultation (Shura). It set the foundation for Islamic governance and jurisprudence.

Caliphs

  • Abu Bakr al-Siddiq (632–634 CE)
  • Umar ibn al-Khattab (634–644 CE)
  • Uthman ibn Affan (644–656 CE)
  • Ali ibn Abi Talib (656–661 CE)
